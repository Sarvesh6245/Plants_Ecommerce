Find about your plant



DAISY


Scientific name: Bellis perennis
Type: Flowering plant
Sunlight: Sun-loving plant
Watering requirements: Water 1-2 times per week
Preferred fertilizers: Phosphorus-rich fertilizer

Daisies are composite flowers, they are actually two flowers that have blended together in complete harmony. So, they have a meaning of true love.

ROSE


Scientific name: Rosa
Type: Flowering plant
Sunlight: Needs sunlight
Watering requirements: 1 or 2 days
Preferred fertilizers: Phosphorus-rich fertilizer

Roses belong to the Rosaceae family, and there are about 360 species in the genus Rosa. Erect bushes with stems are their defining characteristics.

ORCHIDS


Scientific name: Orchidaceae
Type: Flowering plant
Sunlight: Sun-loving plant
Watering requirements: Water 1-2 times per week
Preferred fertilizers: orchid bark mix

The structure of the orchid flower is a key characteristic in identifying them i.e. both halves of the flower mirror each other.

HIBISCUS


Scientific name:
Hibiscus rosa-sinensis
Type: Flowering plant
Sunlight: Sun-loving plant
Suitable soil : Loamy Soil
Watering requirements: Keep the soil moist but not soggy
Preferred fertilizers: N-P-K ratio 12-4-8 or 17-5-24

MARIGOLD


Scientific name: Tagetes
Type: Flowering plant
Sunlight: Sun-loving plant
Watering requirements: Moist soil
Preferred fertilizers: Phosphorus-rich fertilizer

Marigolds have cheery, pom-pom, anemone, or daisy-shaped inflorescences in colors ranging from yellow and gold to orange, red, and mahogany.

WHEAT


Scientific name: Triticum
Type: Crops
Sunlight: Full day
Watering requirements: 350 – 600 mm of water
Preferred fertilizers: Potash

Wheat is typically milled into flour which is then used to make a wide range of foods including bread, sweet and savoury snack foods and confectionery (e.g. liquorice).

RICE


Scientific name: Oryza sativa
Type: Crops
Sunlight: Full daylight
Watering requirements: 350 – 600 mm of water
Preferred fertilizers: Ammonium sulphate rich

Rice is the seed of a semi-aquatic grass that is cultivated extensively in warm climates for its edible grain. It is a staple food throughout the world.
